## 1. Marketing — where a solo studio's hours pay off

**Spend ~70% here (Tier 1, highest ROI/hour):**
- **Referrals + past clients** — referred leads convert 30–50% better than any channel. Ask every happy client directly.
- **Partnerships / white-label** — agencies, photographers, branding & marketing freelancers who send pre-qualified work repeatedly. Best fit for limited time.
- **Local SEO + Google Business Profile (Pasadena)** — the Map "Local Pack" captures ready-to-buy local searchers. One-time setup, compounding.

**Pick ONE, stay consistent (Tier 2):**
- **One social channel** — Instagram (your visual/motion craft) *or* LinkedIn (B2B/higher-ticket). Not both. Weekly competence, not virality.
- **Shopify Partner Program** — free; recurring commission + referral bonus. Join day one for the Shopify side.

**Low priority (Tier 3):** Dribbble/Behance = credibility, weak leads. Clutch/DesignRush = later, once you have reviews.
**Avoid:** Upwork/Fiverr (race-to-the-bottom, opposite of your craft positioning); spec/free work.

**Best-paying niches** (bad existing sites + high client value): home services, dental/medical, legal.

**90-day plan (~5–7 hrs/wk):** ① setup (GBP, Shopify Partner, pick niche + channel) → ② tap network/past clients + 5 partnership notes → ③ weekly content + 3–5 *personalized* outreaches → ④ ask delivered clients for referral + review.

**Funnel metrics:** leads → call → proposal → close. Healthy proposal→close = **25–35%** (40%+ if well-qualified). Log referral source + time/lead per channel.

## 2. Compliant cold outreach (the "businesses with bad/no sites" play)

**Legal bottom line:** US→US B2B cold email *is* legal (CAN-SPAM, opt-out — no prior consent needed). Real risks = domain reputation + platform bans. Stay low-volume + personalized + local.

**Find them:** Google Maps (#1 — search "[trade] Pasadena", flag no/broken/dated sites) · in-person/networking (BNI, Chamber, SBDC — highest local conversion) · Yelp/YellowPages as a *manual* signal only (don't scrape) · IG local hashtags · Nextdoor (visibility).

**CAN-SPAM musts:** accurate headers · honest subject · disclose it's commercial · real physical postal address · working opt-out honored within 10 business days. (CASL/Canada = opt-in, don't cold-email Canadians. GDPR = only EU residents; keep the list EU-free.)

**Protect your domain:** use a **separate sending domain** (not lgrwebstudios.com), warm it ~30 days, set **SPF + DKIM + DMARC**, cap **15–25/day/inbox**, keep spam complaints <0.1%.

**Personalized > spray:** 21–50-recipient sequences reply ~6.2% vs ~2.4% for 500+. Do **10–15 hand-picked/week**, not blasts.

**Workflow:** find (Google Maps) → diagnose the specific flaw (not mobile-friendly, no SSL, slow, dated) → optional 2-min Loom walkthrough (pure value) → one compliant message → 1–2 follow-ups → stop.

**Message structure (personalize, don't template):** subject = specific & honest ("Quick note on [Business]'s site") → line 1: proof you actually looked → line 2: what the gap costs them → line 3: soft offer ("want a free 2-min walkthrough?") → footer: name, LGR Web Studio, physical address, one-line opt-out.

**Channels + etiquette:** email (compliant, low-vol) · LinkedIn (≤15–20 personalized requests/day) · phone/in-person (strongest for local) · IG DM (5–10/day, aged account, no link in first msg).

## 3. Positioning + a portfolio that converts

**Position on CRAFT, not just industry.** Niche = your *premium motion / custom WebGL* specialty (specialist pricing power without shrinking the market). NN/g 2026: generic UI is being automated — differentiate on judgment + outcomes, not decoration. Frame motion/WebGL as conversion + brand-memorability, not eye-candy.

**Pricing:** 3 tiers, **anchor high** (lead with the priciest so the middle looks attractive), "starting at" labels, frame on **ROI not cost**, avoid hourly. Tiers should change *value/outcomes*, not page count.

**Case-study anatomy (the spine of the `work` page):**
`Headline (with the win) → Context/client → Problem → Approach (2–3 key decisions, show the motion/WebGL) → Result (a metric) → Testimonial (name+title+photo) → benefit CTA`
- **Lead with the outcome**, not process ("increased conversion 34%" > "I designed this").
- **3–5 deep case studies** (~500–800 words each), at least one per service.
- **Live link + before/after + crisp screenshots.**
- **Testimonials at the point of doubt** (next to results / before CTA) — can lift conversion 34–69%.
- **Benefit-led CTA** ("Get a site that converts" + Calendly), placed after proof.
- **Studio-wide proof strip** (logos/quotes) above the final CTA.

**Mistakes to avoid:** no metrics · no/buried CTA · too many projects (curate to ~4) · low-res images · no problem/approach context.

**Steal-from examples:** DaiDesigns (package↔proof mapping) · Ferrotype (before/after, red CTAs) · Diana Lu (craft-forward, interaction-led).

## Shopify Partner revenue share — how you actually get paid

You earn a **recurring revenue share (~15–20%, confirm current rate in the Partner Dashboard)** of a client's Shopify subscription **only if**:
1. The store was **created as a development store from YOUR Partner Dashboard**, then
2. **Transferred** to the client (Partner Dashboard → Stores → Transfer ownership) — *not* a client self-signup, and
3. The client is on a **paid plan**, and
4. You remain **partner of record**, and
5. You've set a **payout method** (PayPal/bank); payouts release at the ~$25 minimum.

**The gotcha (already cost a referral once):** if the client self-signed-up for Shopify and you were only added as staff/collaborator, the recurring share for that store is **$0** and can't be claimed retroactively. The rev share is bound to the dev-store→transfer flow at setup.

**For alsoknownas.la — check now:** Partner Dashboard → **Stores** → alsoknownas → are you listed as **partner of record**? Then **Payouts** → set a payout method. If you built it on your dev store and transferred it, the share is already accruing. (No new "sign-up" needed — you're already a Partner.)

## Ready-to-send: warm outreach (do this FIRST — highest ROI, zero risk)

**A) Past client / network referral ask** (personalize line 1):
> Subject: Quick favor
> Hey [Name] — hope [their site/business] is treating you well. I'm taking on a couple new website projects this month and thought I'd ask people I trust first: know anyone who needs a site built or refreshed? I'll take great care of them like I did for you. Even a quick intro means a lot — thanks either way!

**B) Partnership note** (photographers / branding / marketing freelancers — they have clients who need *web*):
> Subject: Web partner for your clients?
> Hi [Name] — really like your [photography/branding] work for [type] businesses. I build bespoke, fast websites (custom + Shopify) for small brands — the piece your clients often need next. Want a simple two-way referral arrangement? Happy to trade portfolios or hop on a quick call. Big fan either way.

Send 3–5 personalized notes/week. Never blast.

## Google Business Profile setup (free, compounding local SEO)
1. Claim at google.com/business → name **LGR Web Studio**, primary category **Website designer** (+ secondary **Web developer**).
2. **Service-area business** — Pasadena + nearby; hide the address if home-based, set service areas.
3. **NAP consistency** — identical Name/Address/Phone on site footer, socials, every directory.
4. Fill it all: description (mention "Pasadena web design · Shopify · custom sites"), services, hours, website link, contact.
5. **Photos** — work screenshots, logo, a headshot (profiles with photos get more contacts).
6. **Get 3–5 reviews** from past clients (alsoknownas, Savy Colours) — the biggest local-ranking lever.
7. Post occasionally (a recent project / offer). Verify via postcard/phone/video.
